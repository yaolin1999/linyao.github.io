Reading Series 002: Physical mechanisms controlling self-aggregation of convection in idealized numerical modeling simulations

by Allison A. Wing and Kerry Emanuel

Main idea

This paper develops a novel method for quantifying the effects of three diabatic physical processes (shortwave radiation convergence, longwave radiation convergence and surface fluxes) on the genesis, development and maintainance of convective self-aggregation. It is mainly based on the budget equation for the spacial variance of the column-integrated frozen moist static energy (FMSE). It presents the evolution of various physical varibles comprehensively with diagrams both in space and time. It also explains the reasons of such evolution, which includes the explanations for longwave radiative heating by using a simple two-layer model and those for surface fluxes which are divided into three components (a wind-driven section, a surface disequilibrium section and an eddy section).
